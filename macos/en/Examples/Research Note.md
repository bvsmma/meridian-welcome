# The Zettelkasten Method

Zettelkasten (German: "slip box") is a note-taking system developed by sociologist Niklas Luhmann. He wrote over 70 books and 400 papers using it.

---

## Core idea

Every note is **atomic** — one idea per note. Notes are linked, not organized in folders.

**Fleeting notes** — quick captures. Raw thoughts, quotes, observations.

**Literature notes** — notes on what you read. In your own words.

**Permanent notes** — fully formed ideas. Written as if for a reader.

---

## Why it works

Traditional note-taking is **hierarchical** — notes live in folders, under topics.

Zettelkasten is **associative** — notes link to each other across topics. A chemistry note can link to a philosophy note if they share an idea.

---

## Luhmann's numbers

- 90,000 note cards in his physical Zettelkasten
- 70+ books published
- 400+ scholarly articles

---

## Key principles

1. **Atomicity** — one idea per note
2. **Autonomy** — each note makes sense without context
3. **Links over folders** — connect, don't categorize
4. **Write in your own words** — forces understanding
5. **Never delete** — add corrections as new notes
